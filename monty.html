Pneumonia

by Taufeeq Mowzer

  • Pneumonia definition

    -Infection of lung parenchyma distal to terminal bronchioles
    -Associated with clinical and/or radiological evidence of consolidation

  • Microbiology

    -Upper respiratory tract(normal flora)
    -Trachae(divides upper and lower tracts)
    -Lower respiratory tract(physiologically sterile)
    common agents- virus,bacteria, mycobacteria
    Routes:
    endogenous(patients normal flora, microbiome)
    exogenous (Pathogen transmitted from people, environment, zoonosis [airborne/resp droplets])

  • Upper respiratory flora

    Commonly carried bacteria:
    Streptococcus viridans –  Endocarditis
    Diphtheroids  Diphtheria
    Anaerobes  Aspiration pneumonia, lung abcess, empyema
    Neisseria spp.

    Bacterial pathogens that can be carried asymptomatically:
    Neisseria meningitides – Meningococ meningitis, sepsis
    Streptococcus pyogenes – Strep throat  RF and glomerulonephritis
    Streptococcus pneumoniae – Meningitis and pneumonia
    Haemophilus influenza – Meningitis and pneumonia esp COPD
    Corynebacterium diphtheria - Diphtheria
    Moraxella catarrhalis – Pneumonia in COPD

    Organisms associated with colonization as a result of antimicrobial therapy: All associated with HAP
    Klebsiella spp.
    E. coli
    Pseudomonas spp.
    Candida albicans – mostly with prolonged ICU stay  invasive candidiasis

    Contamination: low number of organisms, often mixed populations
    Colonisation: higher number of organisms in the absence of symptoms and signs. May be mixed or pure cultures
    Infection: high number of organisms, often single species dominates.
